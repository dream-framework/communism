The Death of “Theft of the Future”

Abstract. Capitalism evolved from exploiting present labor to pre‑owning the future. We formalize the time‑machine mechanics and show why a systemic break is inevitable.

1) From factory to finance

Classical: \(W=c+v+m\). Late capitalism monetizes future flows now.

2) The time‑machine formula

\( \mathrm{NPV} = \sum_{t=1}^{H} \frac{CF_t}{(1+r)^t} \)

\(CF_t\) = labor‑created cash flows; \(r\) = required return. Society mortgaged to its unborn output.

3) AI bubble as a diagnostic

  • Markets price rights to future automation, not present productivity.
  • Capex outruns measured ROI; hype–reality gap widens.

4) Collapse math

\( r>g \Rightarrow \lim_{H\to\infty} \sum \frac{\text{Future Labor}}{(1+r)^t} = 0. \)

If return demands outrun real growth, the discount machine eats the future.

5) Three limits

  • Physical (finite resources)
  • Social (inequality & debt)
  • Mathematical (unserviceable leverage)

6) Bifurcation

Techno‑feudalism vs Techno‑communism. The continuity: labor‑value logic meets modern finance. The result: a necessary paradigm shift.
