Thesis

We already possess the technical base for democratic techno‑communism: real‑time planning, predictive logistics, verifiable ledgers, and scalable compute. What’s missing is political will. Our aim is simple: prioritize human needs and measurable outcomes over speculative finance.

History is accelerating. Either we design institutions worthy of our tools—or become tools of someone else’s institutions.

Evidence

Finance’s time machine

\( \mathrm{NPV}=\sum_{t=1}^{H} \frac{CF_t}{(1+r)^t}\)

When required returns persistently exceed real growth (r>g), each additional future unit contributes vanishing present value. The long tail of needs is erased in budgets optimized for discounted ROI.

The lesson isn’t “math is evil” — it’s that public goals must not be starved by private discounting. We redirect finance to serve a published plan with auditable KPIs.

Planning 2.0 already exists

  • Forecasting at scale: large retailers and platforms run ML demand planning and real‑time supply chain optimization internally.
  • Open ledgers: modern distributed ledgers make tamper‑evident accounting practical for public oversight.
  • Quantum horizon: qubits encode \(2^N\) states—future optimization capacity grows super‑linearly with hardware.
Examples
  • Amazon’s science org publishes on multi‑horizon demand forecasting.
  • Walmart’s global data platform (Luminate → Scintilla) pushes real‑time insights to suppliers.
  • Public‑sector DPI and blockchain pilots prove verifiable registries and entitlements are feasible.

What We Build

Plan 2.0

  • Telemetry‑driven resource planning (energy, logistics, housing, health).
  • Public KPIs and objective functions aligned to needs.
  • Rolling horizons (12 weeks, 12 months, 10 years), with scenario stress‑tests.

Open Ledger

  • Citizen privacy by default; asymmetric transparency for office‑holders.
  • Project budgets, tenders, and delivery milestones on a verifiable log.
  • Programmable audits; immutable decision trails.

CLE (Competency Labor Equivalent)

A contribution index that unlocks rare opportunities (missions, expeditions, advanced study). Basics are universal.

  • Baseline CLE 5.0–6.0 → guaranteed essentials.
  • High CLE 7.0+ → access to rare resources.

Governance for Service

  1. 10+ years of measurable impact; CLE ≥ 7.0.
  2. Psychometrics + fMRI‑style motive testing.
  3. One sandbox year under AI audit (ledgered decisions).
  4. Public dossier review + vote.

Safeguards

  • Rights: universal floor (housing, health, education, nutrition).
  • Data: strict privacy for citizens; full transparency for office‑holders.
  • Checks: independent red‑team, reproducible metrics, public error budgets.

Deployment Path

  1. Pilots: cities/regions for logistics, energy dispatch, and urban flows with live KPIs.
  2. Scale‑out: national DPI and open ledgers; mission‑funded R&D.
  3. Institutions: constitutionalize asymmetric transparency and CLE‑based access to rare resources.
